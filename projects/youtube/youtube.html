Why I Built the YouTube Outlier App

As a creator, I’ve always struggled to figure out which of my videos were truly performing well. Views alone are misleading—what about engagement? What about recency? A video with 1M views might seem successful, but if it’s 5 years old and has no likes or comments, is it really helping my channel grow?

I needed a way to identify outliers—videos that were truly exceptional in terms of engagement, views, and relevance. But manually analyzing every video was time-consuming and inaccurate. I knew there had to be a better way.

YouTube analytics can be overwhelming—views alone don’t tell the full story.

That’s why I built the YouTube Outlier App. I leveraged artificial intelligence to develop a web app that analyzes your videos and scores them based on views, likes, comments, and recency. The problem, ideation, and solution came from my own struggles as a creator. This app is built by a creator, for creators.

The app lets you choose between Popularity, Engagement, or Balanced scoring to align with your goals. It also adjusts for recency, so older videos don’t unfairly dominate your rankings. Using Z-scores, it identifies videos that are statistically significant outliers—helping you focus on what truly matters.

The YouTube Outlier App helps you find your best-performing videos with ease.
